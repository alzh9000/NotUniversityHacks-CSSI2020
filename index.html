Predicting Drought

The Problem

The Drought Crisis

Droughts are a result of extremely dry weather conditions, which often result in a lack of water supply. Due to global warming, the increase in temperature has had a major effect on the frequent occurrence of droughts, especially in areas with warmer climates. Water is a necessity for human survival meaning we must make a great effort in saving as much as we can.

How Bad Is It?

As of 2020, moderate to extreme drought covers 12.3% of the United States. On average, people in America only need about 13 gallons of water a day, but many use up to 150 gallons per day! Nearly 95% of the water we use daily ends up going down the drain. This is water that can’t be put towards farming, drinking, or bathing.

Solution

Introducing: Dropedictor

Dropredictor is an easily accessible and efficient drought predicting web application. When you sign up for an account with Dropredictor, you're signing up for updates on the drought conditions in your state! Using compiled data, it also offers predictions of when the next anticipated drought in your state is going to be, while also offering you tips on what the best ways to save water are.

Features

Location

Pick your location from a list or allow us to find it for you! We’ll be using it to identify when droughts occurred in your area in the past.

Countdown

Based on your location, we’ll calculate when you’ll next be likely to see a drought and let you know how much time you have left!

Tips

We’ll provide you with some tips to save water and prevent future droughts!
